Empanadas de Queso

Description

A typical preparation in Chilean cuisine, consisting of salty dough and cheese. Chileans do eat them throughout the year, but they are most commonly consumed during 18 de Septiembre: the commemoration of the proclamation of the First Government Body of 1810.

Ingredients

  • 1kg all purpose flour
  • 150g shortening
  • 3 cups tepid water
  • 1tbsp salt
  • shredded mozzarella
  • 2 cups of oil for frying

Steps

  1. Melt the shortening in a pan until completely dissolved
  2. In a bowl, mix the flour with the salt
  3. Add the melted shortening to the bowl and mix until combined
  4. Pour the water and mix with a spatula until a dough has formed
  5. Flour the working surface and knead the dough until smooth
  6. Let rest at room temperature for 20 minutes
  7. Divide the dough in 120g balls
  8. Roll each ball into a flat circle using a rolling pin
  9. Add the desired amount of shredded cheese in the centre
  10. Dip your fingers in water and trace the outer half of the circle with them
  11. Fold the other half over and press together so as to seal the dough
  12. Heat a large skillet with 2 cups of sunflower oil
  13. Fry for 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown
  14. Remove from the oil and let cool for 15 minutes before serving
  15. Gently pat each empanada with a piece of paper towel to remove excess oil
